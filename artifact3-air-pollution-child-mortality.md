# Global Air Pollution and Child Mortality Prediction

## Introduction

This project examined the relationship between air pollution and child mortality across multiple countries using statistical and predictive modeling techniques.

## Description

The analysis investigated environmental and socioeconomic factors affecting child mortality rates from 2010 to 2022. Multiple datasets were combined to evaluate the impact of PM2.5 exposure, GDP, healthcare access, and urbanization.

## Objective

To identify key drivers of child mortality and develop predictive models that support evidence-based public health decision-making.

## Process

1. Collected international environmental and health datasets.
2. Cleaned and integrated data from multiple sources.
3. Conducted exploratory data analysis.
4. Applied regression and tree-based machine learning models.
5. Evaluated predictor importance.
6. Developed visualizations and dashboards.
7. Communicated findings and recommendations.
